HOW DIFFERENT WINES ARE MADE?

Great wine begins with great grapes.

\

Winemaking is the production of wine, starting with the selection of fruit: Grapes, and fermentation into alcohol. Red wine and White wine are the main categories of wine. For a wine to be considered dry, it has to have less than 1% residual sugar. On the other hand, sweet wine has a relatively higher residual sugar of above 20%.

In general, the average concentrations of the major components of wine are 85% of water, 13% of alcohol, 1% of Glycerol, 0.5% of acids, 0.4% of Carbohydrates and 0.1% of Phenolics. Red wines are obtained by the alcoholic fermentation of the presence of the skins and seeds of a red grapes, while white wine are exclusively produced by the fermentation of grape juice.

WHO IS THE MOST POPULAR HERE?

Rare Wine for Rare Class.

Pinot Noir is the world's most popular light-bodied red wine. It's loved for its red fruit, flower and spice aromas that are accentuated by a long, smooth finish. While on the other hand, Chardonnay is the most popular white wine on earth. Chardonnay is a green-skinned grape variety used in the production of white wine. The variety originated in the Burgundy of eastern France, but is now grown wherever wine is produced (wine cellar ).

HMMMM...I HEARD WINE IS EXPENSIVE

A great wine for a great price.

Based on statistics posted on vivino, an average bottle of red wine cost $15.66 USD while a very good bottle of wine costs $32.48 USD. Besides, a quality white wine tend to be little expensive, an average white wine costs $14.41 USD while a high quality white wine costs $24.94 USD on average. However, according to the dataset, both wines that have highest price overall (Cabernet Franc) and highest average price (Cabernet Sauvignon) are both red wines which do not match with the statistics above.
